ACADEMIC WRITING ASSIGNMENT
Instructions
• This assignment will be worth 50% of the module marks.
• This is not a group assignment
• This is designed to test your knowledge and understanding of the lectures on Critical
Scientific Reading and Critical Scientific Writing
• There is a 2000 word limit for this assignment. However, this does not include
references, figures, tables and table/figure captions.
• The deadline will be 8 December 2023 at 12 noon.
In this assignment you will be using the paper that has been assigned to you by your
Academic Tutor. This can be found in the paper repository: Assessment Guides > Academic
Writing Assessment > Your Assigned Paper > Your Academic Tutor’s Name – Paper
You are required to read the paper thoroughly and produce an essay based on the format
below:
INTRODUCTION
Suggested length: ~500 words
• This should contain the background of the topic and the research question.
Make sure you introduce the topic in a clear and concise manner.
• Your target audience are undergraduates like yourself.
• You are also encouraged to carry out further reading and discuss the broader implications
of the topic.
• Please make sure you appropriately cite relevant work (in the University of Reading
format: http://libguides.reading.ac.uk/citing-references)
Remember, this assignment should be written in an academic style.
As such, you should avoid:
w clichés ("the flaws in this argument stand out like a sore thumb")
w contractions ("don't", "aren't", "it's")
w phrases that sound like speech ("well, this bit is really fascinating")
w subjective descriptions ("this beautiful sculpture")
w writing in the first person unless instructed too
w using overly complicated language
Please refer to Lecture 4 (Critical Scientific Reading) and Lecture 5 (Critical Scientific
Writing) for more information on the appropriate reading and writing style (the slides,
recording and supplementary material can be found on Blackboard).

CRITICAL ANALYSIS
Suggested length: ~750 words
In this section you should summarise the main findings and critically analyse the paper.
You can consider but not limit yourself to the following:
• What are the results?
• What are the strengths and weaknesses of the research?
• What are the uncertainties and limitations of the work?
• Is there sufficient analysis of the experimental data?
• Have the author(s) considered the work of other researchers in the field?
• Are the conclusions justified?
Please use the material (from Autumn, Week 7 and Autumn, Week 8) and the various
resources on Blackboard for further guidance.
FUTURE RESEARCH & CONCLUSION
Suggested length: ~750 words
In this section you will have to suggest two to three experiments that can be used to further
investigate the topic. These experiments may be related to your critical analysis (i.e. to
address the flaws and/or shortcomings identified in the critical analysis). Structure your
suggestions in this style:
• Aims of the future experiments
• Suggested methodology
There is no need to write the full protocol - just a brief description and the scientific
background of the method should suffice.
• How the anticipated results would further the knowledge of the topic (i.e. how this will
build on what has been done)
End the essay with an appropriate conclusion, considering the broader impact of this research.
